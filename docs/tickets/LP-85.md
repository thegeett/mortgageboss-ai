## FHA Property is the Most Distinctively-FHA Content (no Conventional analog)

A Conventional appraisal targets market **value**; an FHA appraisal ALSO verifies the property meets the
Minimum Property Requirements / Standards for **safety, security, and soundness** (the three S's). A property
failing any of the three S's needs **repairs before FHA insures** the loan (II.D). MPR = existing construction;
MPS = new construction (24 CFR 200.926).

## The "Subject-to-Repair" Conditional Model (reuses LP-84's mitigable pattern)

When the appraiser finds an MPR deficiency, the appraisal is issued "subject to" the repair — and **most
deficiencies are correctable** (fix, re-inspect, proceed). So MPR findings are **MITIGABLE**, not silent hard
blocks:

- **Correctable → YELLOW** (a subject-to-repair finding resolvable by documenting the repair/re-inspection via
  LP-75's resolution — OVERRIDDEN-with-reason / APPLIED): roof, lead paint, functional systems, water
  intrusion, handrails, well/septic, the three-S's umbrella.
- **Un-correctable → RED** (a harder block): `mpr_bedroom_egress` (a no-egress bedroom), `mpr_defective_conditions`
  (serious structural failure), and the missing FHA appraisal (`doc.fha_appraisal_present`, the Tier-2 anchor).

## TIER-2 Honesty (the critical posture — same as LP-77's appraised value)

Most MPR conditions (peeling paint, roof, handrails, water intrusion) are **observed by the appraiser** and live
in the appraisal document — Tier-2 (manual / not deterministically extracted). These rules therefore do **NOT**
pretend to detect physical deficiencies. They:

1. check the **FHA appraisal is present** (the RED anchor the MPR rules depend on),
2. check whether it is **"subject to" repairs** (where that datum is captured), and
3. **surface the MPR checklist** for human/appraiser confirmation — each deficiency rule reads an
   appraiser-provided fact and is recorded **not-evaluated** (graceful) until that datum is captured.

We don't fake what the system can't see; the appraiser/AI provides the observation, the engine tracks the gate.

## MPRs are Policy-in-Flux

FHA published a **2026 Request for Information** to modernize the MPRs (no comprehensive update in 20+ years;
comment period through June 2026). So the content is not only lender-overlay-subject but **actively under
revision** — the rules carry a "subject to the pending MPR modernization" caveat on top of the starter marker.

## Program-Gating + Applicability-Gating

of them. Applicability gates: construction status (new → MPS), property type (condo), well/septic presence,
pre-1978 (lead paint), subject-to-repair status. The Conventional test file (Mahesh) never triggers these.

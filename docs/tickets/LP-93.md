# LP-93 — Normalized-substance finding identity + dedup

- **Ticket:** LP-93 (second ticket of the finding-presentation epic, LP-92..98)
- **Epic:** Phase 3.5 — Finding presentation
- **Status:** Completed
- **Date:** 2026-06-30

## Summary

The same discrepancy worded two ways showed as **two** Open findings. LP-93 gives every finding
a **normalized-substance identity** (canonical type + subject value(s), case-folded and
punctuation-canonicalized — deterministic textual, NO fuzzy) and dedups on it at emission, so
same-substance-different-wording collapses to one, genuinely-different subjects stay separate,
and a re-detected resolved finding keeps its resolution. See **ADR-218**.

## The bug (live repro)

Two Open findings on Mahesh's file, identical except case + dash:

- "Documented employer not among the stated employers: **Thermofisher Life Science – PPD
  Development LP.**" (en-dash)
- "Documented employer not among the stated employers: **THERMOFISHER LIFE SCIENCE — PPD
  DEVELOPMENT LP.**" (em-dash)

Same rule (`xsrc.income.employer_name_consistency`), same employer, same substance.

## Root cause (from the trace)

- Both cross-source emission paths supersede the file's OPEN findings + preserve RESOLVED ones,
  then re-emit — `app/services/cross_source.py` (AI) and
  `app/services/cross_source_deterministic.py` (`xsrc.*`).
- `_check_employer_name_consistency` fires once **per documented-employer string**; two
  documents carried the same employer with different case + dash.
- The rule's comparison key `_norm` (`rules.py:102`) folds case + whitespace but **not** the
  en-dash/em-dash, so the two `subject_key`s differed → two matches → two findings.
- There was **no normalized-substance dedup at emission** — within-run wording variants (and a
  re-detected resolved finding) duplicated.

## The fix

`app/services/finding_identity.py` (new) — the single, shared identity + dedup:

- `normalize_text(s)` — NFKC → dash variants (`‐‑‒–—―−`) → `-`, curly quotes → straight,
  case-fold, whitespace-collapse. **Deterministic textual only; no fuzzy matching.**
- `finding_identity(finding)` → `(normalize_text(type|rule), normalize_text(subject))`, where
  the subject is `details.subject_key` (deterministic) or `stated_value`/`document_value` (AI).
- `existing_identities(db, loan_file_id)` — the identities of the file's live findings (the
  dedup seed; includes the preserved resolved ones), tenant-scoped by the file id.

Wired into **both** emission loops:

- `run_cross_source_deterministic` — seed `seen` from `existing_identities` (after supersede),
  skip a fresh finding whose identity is already present (the rule still marks its canonical
  type fired, so the AI defer is unchanged — only persistence dedups).
- `run_cross_source` (AI loop) — same `seen`/skip, seeded after the deterministic set is emitted
  (so an AI finding restating an existing discrepancy collapses). Added an `ai_deduped` log
  counter.

## Behavior

- **Collapse:** two casings/dashes of one employer → **one** finding (the first kept, its wording
  preserved).
- **No over-collapse:** two different employers / amounts / documents / types → **separate**
  findings; "Thermofisher" vs "Thermo Fisher Scientific" (a real textual difference) stay
  separate (no fuzzy).
- **Resolution preserved:** a resolved finding re-detected (same normalized identity) is skipped
  → keeps its resolution, not reopened, not duplicated.
- **Uniform** across deterministic (`xsrc.*`) and AI findings.

## Tests

`backend/tests/services/test_finding_identity_dedup.py` (10):
- `normalize_text` folds case/dash/quote/whitespace; keeps real textual differences.
- `finding_identity` collapses case/dash variants; separates different subjects.
- The live repro collapses to one (first wording kept); different employers stay two; no fuzzy
